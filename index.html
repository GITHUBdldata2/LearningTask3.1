My Top 10 Favorites (Food Edition)


Top 1 - Lasagna

Lasagna is an Italian dish of pasta sheets layered with fillings and baked. It's often made with meat, cheese, tomato sauce, and vegetables.

Top 2 - Hotdogs

A hot dog is a cooked sausage served in a bun. Hot dogs are made from a variety of meats, including beef, pork, chicken, and turkey. They are often seasoned with garlic, salt, sugar, and other spices.

Top 3 - Sushi

Sushi is a Japanese dish of vinegared rice and various ingredients like fish, vegetables, or egg. The word "sushi" comes from a Japanese word that means "sour rice".

Top 4 - Onigiri

Onigiri are Japanese rice balls made of short-grain rice, often wrapped in seaweed and filled with savory or salty ingredients. They are a common snack in Japan and are easy to make at home.

Top 5 - Icecream

Ice cream is a frozen dessert made from milk or cream, sugar, and flavorings. It's usually sweet and cold, and melts if it's not kept cold.

Top 6 - Corndogs

A corn dog is a hot dog on a stick that's coated in cornmeal batter and deep-fried. It's a popular American fast food and street food.

Top 7 - Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a traditional Filipino vegetable stew that's made with a variety of vegetables and a savory sauce. The name comes from the Ilocano word for "shrunk" or "shriveled".

Top 8 - Baby back ribs

Baby Back Ribs: Also called back ribs or loin ribs, baby back ribs are the most popular cut of pork ribs. They're called “baby back” not because they come from baby pigs but rather because they're smaller than other rib cuts and come from the section of the ribs closer to the back of the pig.

Top 9 - Sisisg

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish of chopped, fried pig parts and chicken liver, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It's often served on a sizzling plate.

Top 10 - Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a long, thin, cylindrical pasta that's a staple of Italian cuisine. It's made from durum wheat semolina or flour and water.
